# Chapter 14: Career Management

This directory contains practical templates and tools for Chapter 14 of "Fundamentals of Software Engineering," 
which covers career planning, professional development, and navigating your software engineering career.

## Overview

This chapter helps you take control of your career journey. Unlike other chapters with code examples, 
this one provides practical documents and templates you can use immediately to plan your career path, 
track your achievements, and prepare for interviews.

## Available Templates

### 1. `personal-tech-radar.md`
**Purpose:** Strategic learning and skill prioritization
- Technology radar template with Adopt/Trial/Assess/Hold categories
- Helps you focus on technologies aligned with your career goals
- Review quarterly to stay current and strategic

### 2. `wins-document-template.md`
**Purpose:** Track achievements and build confidence
- Simple template for recording professional wins
- Valuable for performance reviews, resumes, and interviews
- Combat imposter syndrome with concrete evidence of your growth

### 3. `career-roadmap-template.md`
**Purpose:** Long-term career planning
- Set 1-year, 3-year, and 5-year goals
- Break down goals into actionable milestones
- Identify skill gaps and create a plan to address them

### 4. `interview-prep-checklist.md`
**Purpose:** Interview preparation and execution
- Comprehensive checklist for before, during, and after interviews
- Company research guide
- Common questions to prepare for

## How to Use These Templates

1. **Start with the career roadmap** - Define where you want to be
2. **Build your tech radar** - Align your learning with your goals
3. **Track your wins** - Document achievements as they happen
4. **Prepare for interviews** - Use the checklist when opportunities arise

## Key Learning Objectives

After using these templates, you should be able to:

1. **Plan Your Career Path**
   - Identify what you're passionate about
   - Understand various career options (technical expert, leadership, alternative paths)
   - Create actionable goals and milestones
   - Practice deliberate skill acquisition

2. **Walk Your Career Path**
   - Document and celebrate your achievements
   - Overcome imposter syndrome with evidence of growth
   - Build and maintain a professional network
   - Ace interviews through preparation and practice
   - Create work-life balance

## Getting Started

1. Copy any template to your personal note-taking system
2. Fill it out with your current information
3. Set reminders to review and update regularly
4. Keep these documents private and update them continuously

## Key Takeaways

- **Own your career**: You are responsible for your career progression
- **Be strategic**: Focus on skills that align with your goals
- **Document everything**: Your wins are easy to forget without records
- **Build relationships**: Your network is as valuable as your skills
- **Stay balanced**: A sustainable career requires work-life balance
- **Embrace growth**: Imposter syndrome often means you're pushing yourself
