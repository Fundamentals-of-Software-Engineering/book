# Interview Preparation Checklist

A comprehensive guide for acing your next software engineering interview. 
Complete each section as you prepare.

---

## Phase 1: Before Applying

### Company Research
- [ ] Company mission, values, and recent news (last 6-12 months)
- [ ] Products and services they offer
- [ ] Tech stack and engineering blog
- [ ] Company size, funding, and growth stage
- [ ] Glassdoor/employee reviews
- [ ] Recent product launches or acquisitions

### Role Analysis
- [ ] Read job description carefully
- [ ] Identify required vs. nice-to-have skills
- [ ] Match your experience to their needs
- [ ] Note technologies you need to review
- [ ] Identify gaps in your knowledge

### Network Check
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for connections at the company
- [ ] Reach out for insider insights
- [ ] Ask about interview process and culture
- [ ] Request informational interviews if possible

---

## Phase 2: After Getting Interview

### Deep Research (1-2 weeks before)
- [ ] Study their tech stack in detail
- [ ] Read engineering blog posts
- [ ] Review GitHub repos if public
- [ ] Understand their main competitors
- [ ] Research your interviewers on LinkedIn
- [ ] Prepare 5-7 questions to ask them

### Technical Preparation
- [ ] Review fundamentals: data structures, algorithms
- [ ] Practice coding challenges (if applicable)
- [ ] Review system design concepts
- [ ] Refresh knowledge of listed technologies
- [ ] Review your own projects and wins document

### Practice Common Questions

#### General Questions
- [ ] "Tell me about yourself" (2-minute pitch)
- [ ] "Why are you leaving your current role?"
- [ ] "Why do you want to work here?"
- [ ] "Where do you see yourself in 3-5 years?"
- [ ] "What's your salary expectation?"

#### Technical Questions
- [ ] "Walk me through debugging a production issue"
- [ ] "Describe a challenging technical problem you solved"
- [ ] "How do you stay current with technology?"
- [ ] "How do you approach code reviews?"
- [ ] "Design a [simple system like URL shortener]"

### Mock Interviews
- [ ] Schedule 2-3 practice sessions
- [ ] Practice with a peer or mentor
- [ ] Try AI-powered mock interviews (voice mode)
- [ ] Record yourself and review
- [ ] Get feedback on technical explanations

---

## Phase 3: Questions to Ask Them

### Team & Culture
- [ ] What does a typical day look like?
- [ ] How does the team handle disagreements?
- [ ] How do you handle work-life balance?

### Technical
- [ ] What's your development process?
- [ ] How do you approach technical debt?
- [ ] What's your testing strategy?
- [ ] How do you make architectural decisions?

### Growth
- [ ] What does success look like in the first 90 days?
- [ ] How do you support professional development?
- [ ] What's the career progression path?
- [ ] Are there mentorship opportunities?

### Practical
- [ ] What's the remote/hybrid policy?
- [ ] Any travel requirements?
- [ ] What's the typical timeline for a decision?

---

## Phase 4: Day Before Interview

### Logistics
- [ ] Confirm date, time, and format (in-person/virtual)
- [ ] Know interviewer names and roles
- [ ] Plan your route/commute (arrive 15 min early)
- [ ] Test video/audio for virtual interviews
- [ ] Prepare your workspace (clean background)

### Materials
- [ ] Print 3-5 copies of resume
- [ ] Prepare notepad and pen
- [ ] Review your wins document
- [ ] Review company research notes
- [ ] Have questions ready to ask

### Self-Care
- [ ] Get good night's sleep
- [ ] Prepare interview outfit
- [ ] Plan a healthy meal before

---

## Phase 5: Day of Interview

### Pre-Interview (In-Person)
- [ ] Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- [ ] Silence phone notifications
- [ ] Visit restroom
- [ ] Take deep breaths, visualize success

### Pre-Interview (Virtual)
- [ ] Log in 5 minutes early
- [ ] Close unnecessary apps
- [ ] Silence all notifications
- [ ] Have water nearby
- [ ] Check camera/mic one last time

### During Interview
- [ ] Greet interviewer professionally
- [ ] Make eye contact and smile
- [ ] Listen carefully to questions
- [ ] Think before answering
- [ ] Clarify if you don't understand
- [ ] Explain your thought process (especially for coding)
- [ ] Use specific examples from wins document
- [ ] Ask your prepared questions
- [ ] Show genuine enthusiasm
- [ ] Thank them for their time

---

## Phase 6: After Interview

### Immediate (Same Day)
- [ ] Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Mention specific conversation points
- [ ] Reaffirm your interest
- [ ] Address any questions you missed

### Reflection
- [ ] What went well?
- [ ] What could improve?
- [ ] Questions that caught you off guard?
- [ ] Topics to study more?
- [ ] Update your prep notes

### Follow-Up
- [ ] Note when they said they'd respond
- [ ] Set reminder to follow up if no response
- [ ] Keep applying to other roles (don't wait)

---

## If You Get the Offer

- [ ] Review compensation package thoroughly
- [ ] Research market rates for the role
- [ ] Consider total comp (salary, bonus, equity, benefits)
- [ ] Negotiate if appropriate
- [ ] Ask for offer in writing
- [ ] Request reasonable time to decide
- [ ] Evaluate against your career roadmap

---

## If You Don't Get the Offer

- [ ] Thank them for the opportunity
- [ ] Request specific feedback
- [ ] Ask to stay in touch
- [ ] Don't burn bridges
- [ ] Learn from the experience
- [ ] Update your prep materials
- [ ] Keep applying - next one is closer!

---

## Sample Thank-You Email

```
Subject: Thank you - [Position] Interview

Hi [Interviewer Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today to discuss the
[Position] role at [Company]. I enjoyed learning about [specific topic
discussed] and was particularly excited to hear about [specific project
or initiative].

Our conversation reinforced my interest in joining the team. I'm
confident my experience with [relevant skill/project] would allow me
to contribute to [specific team goal or project].

Please let me know if you need any additional information. I look
forward to hearing from you about next steps.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Emergency Prep (Last Minute)

If you have < 24 hours to prepare:

**Priority 1 (1 hour):**
- [ ] Research company basics
- [ ] Review job description
- [ ] Prepare "tell me about yourself"
- [ ] Review your wins document
- [ ] Prepare 3 questions to ask

**Priority 2 (2 hours):**
- [ ] Practice common questions
- [ ] Review fundamentals of listed tech
- [ ] Test tech setup (if virtual)

**Priority 3 (If time allows):**
- [ ] Deep dive on company tech/products
- [ ] Practice coding challenges
- [ ] Mock interview with AI

---

## Remember

- **Preparation builds confidence**: The more you prepare, the less nervous you'll be
- **They want you to succeed**: Interviewers are rooting for you
- **It's a two-way street**: You're interviewing them too
- **Rejection is data**: Use feedback to improve for next time
- **Network matters**: Every interview is a networking opportunity
